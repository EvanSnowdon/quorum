# Disclaimer & Compliance Notice

The skills in this library encode **general analytical frameworks** so that an AI
agent can apply them rigorously. Please read the following before use.

## 1. What these skills are

Each skill is a community-compiled, plain-text procedure for *applying* a
published analytical framework (for example, an industry-structure analysis or a
market-sizing method). The skills capture the **method** — the questions to ask,
the steps to follow, and the evidence standard to hold — not the prose, figures,
exhibits, or datasets of any book, article, report, or course.

## 2. Frameworks only — not the underlying works

These skills describe the **publicly known, generally taught form** of each
framework. They do **not** reproduce, excerpt, or substitute for the original
books, articles, lectures, or proprietary materials in which a framework was
first presented or is sold. The original works contain explanation, examples,
and depth that these short procedures deliberately do not. **If you want the full
treatment, buy and read the source.** Each skill names its canonical source in
its frontmatter and links here.

## 3. No affiliation and no endorsement

The frameworks referenced are associated with their respective authors,
institutions, and publishers. This project, its maintainers, and its
contributors are **not affiliated with, sponsored by, endorsed by, or connected
to** any of those authors, institutions, firms, or publishers. References to a
named framework, author, or organization are **nominative** — used only to
identify the framework being applied — and imply no relationship or approval.

## 4. No impersonation of any person

These skills are tools for analysis. They are written in a neutral,
methodological voice and **do not impersonate, speak as, or claim to channel any
living or deceased individual.** A skill that applies a framework associated with
a particular thinker is not that thinker's voice, opinion, or work product, and
must not be presented as such.

## 5. Trademarks

Framework names, book titles, company names, and product names mentioned in this
library are the **property of their respective owners** and are used for
identification and reference only. No claim is made to any such mark, and no
sponsorship or affiliation is implied by their use.

## 6. Sources and attribution

Where a framework has an identifiable author or canonical text, the relevant
skill cites it (in `metadata.canonical_source` and in the skill body). Some
frameworks (for example, MECE issue trees, PESTEL, or standard discounted-cash-
flow valuation) are widely taught and have **no single proprietary owner**; these
are described as general practice and attributed to the body of public teaching
on the topic rather than to one source.

## 7. Not professional advice

Output produced with these skills is **decision-support, not professional advice.**
It is not investment, legal, accounting, tax, or other regulated advice, and it
is not a substitute for due diligence or for a qualified human who owns the
decision. Automated analyses can be wrong; claims tagged `[INFERENCE]` are
explicitly reasoned rather than sourced and should be pressure-tested. Use at
your own risk.

## 8. Accuracy and data

These skills instruct an agent to prefer official, first-party data and to label
every claim `[DATA]` (sourced) or `[INFERENCE]` (reasoned) with a confidence
value. They cannot, however, guarantee the accuracy, completeness, or currency
of any particular output. Verify material facts against primary sources before
relying on them.

## 9. Takedown and corrections

We respect the rights of authors, publishers, and rights-holders. If you are a
rights-holder and believe a skill misrepresents your work, crosses from
describing a framework into reproducing protected expression, or otherwise
raises a concern, **contact the maintainers and we will review and, where
warranted, revise or remove the material promptly.** Open an issue or use the
security/contact channel listed in the repository.

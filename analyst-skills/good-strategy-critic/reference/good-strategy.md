# Good Strategy / Bad Strategy — Reference

## The kernel (the irreducible core of any good strategy)

| Element | Question it answers | What "good" looks like | What "bad/absent" looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diagnosis** | What is really going on? | Names the one or two *critical* challenges; an insightful simplification of a complex situation | A fact dump, or no challenge stated at all |
| **Guiding policy** | How will we cope with the diagnosed challenge? | An overall *approach/method* that creates advantage; rules things in and out | A goal ("be #1"), a slogan, or a list of initiatives |
| **Coherent action** | What concrete, coordinated steps follow? | Mutually reinforcing actions, resourced, that execute the policy | Disconnected actions, or actions that contradict each other |

The kernel test: if you cannot quote all three from the material, it is not yet a strategy.

## The four signatures of bad strategy

1. **Fluff** — superficial restatement dressed in inflated buzzwords and esoteric concepts to create the illusion of expertise. *Detection:* strip the jargon; if nothing concrete remains, it's fluff. ("Customer-centric, AI-enabled, synergistic ecosystem.")
2. **Failure to face the challenge** — strategy that doesn't define the obstacle. *Detection:* ask "what is the difficulty this is designed to overcome?" If unanswerable, the strategy cannot be evaluated or improved.
3. **Mistaking goals for strategy** — expressions of desire substituted for a plan to overcome obstacles. *Detection:* every "we will achieve X" with no "by doing Y because Z."
4. **Bad strategic objectives** — objectives that fail to address critical issues, or are impracticable. Two sub-types:
   - **"Dog's dinner"** — a long, unprioritized list of disparate goals (a to-do list, not a strategy).
   - **"Blue-sky" objectives** — restating the desired end state as if naming it achieves it ("achieve operational excellence").

## Sources of power (where good strategy concentrates force)

Good strategy applies relative strength to relative weakness. Look for at least one:
leverage (anticipation + a pivot point + concentration), proximate objectives (close enough to be feasible), chain-link systems (fix the binding link), design (tight coordination), focus, growth from advantage, using advantage, dynamics (riding a wave of change), inertia & entropy (exploiting rivals' rigidity).

## Chain-of-reasoning audit

Walk the logic link by link and find the weakest:
`Diagnosis → Guiding policy → Coordinated actions → Mechanism of advantage → Result`
At each arrow ask: *Does this actually follow, or is there an unstated assumption / a hidden "and then a miracle occurs"?* Name the single weakest link.

## Steelman-then-attack protocol

1. State the **most charitable** version of the strategy (assume competent authors).
2. State the **single most damaging objection** a sharp rival or skeptical board would raise.
3. Decide: is the flaw *structural* (bad strategy) or a *defensible bet you doubt* (risky-but-real)? Label which — fairness is part of credibility.

## Verdict scale

- **Real strategy** — kernel complete, coherent, with identified leverage. Risks are bets, not gaps.
- **Partial** — kernel present but a link is weak, focus is diluted, or one element is thin.
- **Bad strategy** — one or more bad-strategy signatures dominate; no honest diagnosis or no mechanism.

## Worked critique (≈180 words)

**Material under review:** "{region} regional bank, {industry}=retail banking. Strategy: 'Drive sustainable growth and shareholder value by becoming the most customer-centric digital bank, leveraging our strong brand and innovative culture to capture the underbanked.'"

- **Diagnosis** `[INFERENCE] 0.8`: **Absent.** No statement of the critical challenge (e.g., deposit-cost disadvantage vs. incumbents, or distribution gaps in underbanked districts). Cannot be improved without one.
- **Guiding policy** `[INFERENCE] 0.75`: **Absent / fluff.** "Most customer-centric digital bank" is an aspiration, not an approach. ("Leveraging strong brand and innovative culture" = fluff.)
- **Coherent action** `[INFERENCE] 0.8`: **Absent.** No coordinated steps; "capture the underbanked" is a goal.
- **Signatures present** `[INFERENCE] 0.8`: fluff + goal-as-strategy + blue-sky objective.

**Steelman:** if the underbanked segment is genuinely underserved and the bank has a low-cost mobile-onboarding capability rivals lack, there *could* be leverage. **Attack:** none of that is stated, and deposit economics likely make "growth" value-destructive without a cost-of-funds answer.

**Verdict: Bad strategy.** Most important gap: no diagnosis of the binding constraint. One change: replace the slogan with a one-paragraph diagnosis of *why* the underbanked are unserved and *what* specifically this bank can do that incumbents can't.

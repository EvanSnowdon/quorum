# PESTEL — Reference

## Six-factor prompt checklist (keep only industry-relevant items)

**Political** — regime stability; industrial policy & subsidies; state ownership / SOE role; trade policy & tariffs; taxation *policy direction* (vs. current rates, which are Economic); FDI rules; geopolitical exposure; local-content / procurement rules.

**Economic** — GDP growth & cycle; inflation; interest rates; exchange rate; disposable income & consumption; unemployment & labour cost; access to capital / credit conditions; the sector's key input/commodity prices.

**Social** — population size & age structure; urbanization & migration; household formation; education & skills; health & lifestyle trends; values, religion, and category-specific consumer attitudes; class/income distribution.

**Technological** — relevant emerging technologies; rate of change / obsolescence; R&D intensity & public research funding; automation potential; digital infrastructure & internet/smartphone penetration; standards & platform shifts.

**Environmental** — physical climate risk (heat, flood, drought); resource scarcity & water stress; energy transition & emissions targets; circularity/waste rules; consumer sustainability expectations; supply-chain environmental exposure.

**Legal** — sector-specific regulation & licensing; competition/antitrust; labour & employment law; data protection & privacy; IP regime & enforcement; product safety & standards; advertising/consumer-protection law; health & safety.

*Tip:* "current tax rate / current interest rate" = Economic; "the government's stated intention to change them" = Political. Don't double-book.

## Impact × direction × certainty rubric (per retained factor)

| Field | Values | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Direction** | rising / falling / volatile / stable | The trajectory over the horizon |
| **Channel** | demand / cost / entry / legitimacy | *How* it reaches the industry — required |
| **Impact** | Low / Medium / High | Strength of effect on industry economics |
| **Certainty** | high / medium / low | Confidence the change occurs as described |

A factor with no channel stated is not analysis. Impact without certainty hides risk.

## Key-driver prioritization grid (impact × certainty)

|  | Low certainty | High certainty |
|---|---|---|
| **High impact** | **Scenario drivers** — model as uncertainties (hand to scenario/`three-horizons`) | **Key drivers of change** — plan firmly around these |
| **Low impact** | Monitor list | Background — note and move on |

Output should foreground the High-impact row: firm planning assumptions (high certainty) and the named uncertainties (low certainty).

## Trend vs. uncertainty split

- **Trends** — high-certainty directional forces (aging population, an enacted phase-out schedule). Build the base case on them.
- **Uncertainties** — high-impact, low-certainty forces (election outcomes, subsidy renewal, a tech breakthrough). These define the scenario axes — name the top one or two.

## Common errors

- Listing generic macro facts unconnected to the industry (a country briefing, not a sector PESTEL).
- No time horizon → a static snapshot.
- Treating all six groups as equally important (they never are).
- Stating impact without the *channel* (demand/cost/entry/legitimacy).
- Stopping at the list instead of extracting the 3–5 drivers and their "so what."
- Putting firm-level factors (a competitor's move) in PESTEL — those belong in Five Forces.

## Worked example (≈170 words)

**Industry:** Private tutoring / after-school education, {region}.

- **Political** `[INFERENCE] 0.6`: government signals to curb academic-pressure and regulate the sector → High impact, *legitimacy + entry* channel, headwind. (Watch for formal rules → moves to Legal.)
- **Legal** `[DATA] 0.7`: new licensing and advertising restrictions enacted for tutoring providers → High, *entry/cost*, headwind for unlicensed players, consolidation tailwind for compliant ones.
- **Social** `[DATA] 0.7`: falling birth rate shrinking the school-age cohort over 10 yrs → High, *demand*, structural headwind; but intense parental investment per child partially offsets near-term `[INFERENCE] 0.6`.
- **Economic** `[DATA] 0.6`: squeezed household discretionary spend → Medium, *demand*, headwind in downturns.
- **Technological** `[INFERENCE] 0.55`: online/AI tutoring lowering delivery cost and widening reach → Medium, *cost + entry*, mixed (enables low-cost entrants).
- **Environmental** — Low relevance.

**Key drivers:** (1) regulatory tightening [Legal/Political], (2) demographic shrinkage [Social], (3) digital delivery economics [Technological]. **Net direction:** headwind for the traditional in-person model, selective tailwind for licensed/digital players. **Biggest uncertainty:** severity and enforcement of the regulatory regime.

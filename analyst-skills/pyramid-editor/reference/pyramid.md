# Minto Pyramid Principle — Reference

## The pyramid shape

```
                 Governing thought (the answer)
                /            |            \
        Key-line arg 1   Key-line arg 2   Key-line arg 3   (MECE)
         /    \             /   \            /    \
      support support    support support  support support
```

- **Top:** one governing thought — the single answer to the reader's question.
- **Key line:** 2–5 arguments that, together, support the governing thought and are MECE.
- **Support:** data and reasoning under each argument.

Read top-down (the reader gets the answer first); built bottom-up (group facts, induce the message).

## SCQA introduction

Set up the question the document answers before answering it:

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Situation** | A stable, agreed starting context the reader accepts |
| **Complication** | What changed or went wrong — creates tension |
| **Question** | The question the complication raises in the reader's mind |
| **Answer** | The governing thought (top of the pyramid) |

Keep the Situation non-controversial; the reader should nod before the Complication lands.

## MECE grouping

Every group of ideas must be:
- **Mutually Exclusive** — no two items overlap.
- **Collectively Exhaustive** — together they cover the whole space, no gaps.

Test each grouping: could an item belong in two buckets (not ME)? Is there an obvious missing bucket (not CE)? Common MECE structures: by process step, by component/structure, by segment, by cause.

## Horizontal logic: deductive vs inductive

- **Deductive** — a chain: major premise → minor premise → therefore. Use sparingly; it's harder to read at the key-line level.
- **Inductive** — a set of ideas of the *same kind* summarized by one plural noun ("three barriers to entry…"). Usually preferred for the key line.

Within any one grouping, pick one mode. Mixing them is a logic smell.

## Summarize, don't label

The cardinal rule: each headline states the *insight*, not the topic.

- Weak (label): "Costs", "Competition", "Regulation".
- Strong (insight): "Input costs are rising faster than the industry can pass through", "Two incumbents control distribution", "Pending net-metering rules could halve project returns".

If your headers read as a table of contents, the pyramid isn't doing its job. A reader should be able to read only the headlines and get the argument.

## Vertical logic check

Each box must answer the question its parent provokes ("why?"/"how?"), and each parent must summarize its children. Walk down: does each level earn the one above? Walk up: does each group genuinely sum to its header?

## Editing checklist

- One governing thought, stated first.
- SCQA introduction present and the Situation uncontroversial.
- Key line 2–5 items, MECE.
- Headlines are insights, not labels.
- One ordering mode per group.
- Evidence labels (`[DATA]`/`[INFERENCE]`) preserved.
- Gaps and contradictions flagged, not hidden.

## Worked example (≈170 words): before / after

**Before (working-paper dump):** "We looked at suppliers, buyers, rivals, costs, and some regulation. There's a lot going on. Suppliers are concentrated. Buyers have choices. Margins are thin. There may be a new rule. Overall the market is challenging."

**After (pyramid):**

- **Governing thought:** "This market can earn above-cost-of-capital returns only for players who lock in scarce supply — supplier power is the binding constraint." `[INFERENCE] 0.7`
  - **Key line 1:** Supplier concentration caps margins industry-wide. `[DATA] 0.7`
  - **Key line 2:** Buyers add pressure but are not the binding force (fragmented, moderate switching costs). `[INFERENCE] 0.6`
  - **Key line 3:** A pending regulation could shift the constraint within two years — the key uncertainty to monitor. `[DATA] 0.6`

**SCQA intro:** Situation — the market is growing; Complication — yet incumbent margins are compressing; Question — can a new entrant earn attractive returns; Answer — only by securing supply.

**Gap flagged:** no working paper covered substitute inputs — needed for collective exhaustiveness under Key line 1.

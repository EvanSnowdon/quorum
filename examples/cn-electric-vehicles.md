# Electric Vehicles in China — Market Scan

Engagement: `CN × electric vehicles × scan` · Sample deliverable (abridged)

---

## Executive summary

**China's EV market has shifted from a growth race to a consolidation war:
demand keeps expanding, but price compression and excess assembly capacity
mean the strategic question for any entrant is no longer "how to ride the
growth" but "which niche survives consolidation."**

Three findings support this view:

1. **Demand is structural, not subsidy-driven anymore.** New-energy vehicles
   account for roughly half of new passenger-car sales, well past the early-
   adopter chasm; growth is now pulled by replacement cycles and export volume.
   `[DATA] NEV share of new passenger-car sales ~50% (source: CAAM monthly
   releases) [0.85]`
2. **Industry attractiveness is deteriorating for generalists.** Rivalry and
   buyer power rate High under a Five Forces read: 50+ active brands, repeated
   list-price cuts, and dealer inventory pressure. Overall industry
   attractiveness: **2/5**. `[INFERENCE] Price war persists through the
   forecast window given capacity overhang [0.7]`
3. **Defensible positions cluster in the value chain, not the badge.**
   Batteries, thermal systems, and smart-cockpit software hold margin while
   assembly margins compress. `[INFERENCE] Supplier power concentrates in
   cell chemistry and software layers [0.75]`

**Recommendation (scan-level):** enter via the component/software layer or a
clearly bounded vehicle niche (e.g. export-oriented compact EVs); avoid
undifferentiated domestic assembly.

## Macro environment (PESTEL, abridged)

- Political: NEV purchase-tax exemption extended; local content informally
  favored in fleet procurement. `[DATA] Purchase-tax exemption in force
  (source: MOF announcement) [0.9]`
- Economic: household demand cautious; auto credit conditions loose.
  `[INFERENCE] Price sensitivity stays elevated through the cycle [0.7]`
- Technological: LFP chemistry dominates volume segments; fast-charging and
  city-NOA software are the visible differentiators. `[DATA] LFP holds the
  majority of installed battery capacity (source: CABIA data) [0.8]`
- Legal/Environmental: dual-credit policy still binds ICE output; data-
  security rules constrain foreign telematics stacks. `[DATA] Dual-credit
  scheme active (source: MIIT) [0.85]`

## Competitive landscape (Five Forces, abridged)

| Force | Rating | Key evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Rivalry | **High** | 50+ brands; rolling price cuts `[DATA] (source: CPCA pricing trackers) [0.8]` |
| Buyer power | **High** | Low switching costs, dense comparison channels `[INFERENCE] [0.75]` |
| Supplier power | **Medium–High** | Cell supply concentrated among top producers `[DATA] (source: CABIA share data) [0.8]` |
| New entry | **Medium** | Capital intensity up, but tech giants keep entering via software `[INFERENCE] [0.7]` |
| Substitutes | **Low–Medium** | Transit quality high in tier-1 cities; ICE share structurally declining `[INFERENCE] [0.7]` |

**Binding constraint:** rivalry. Strategy must be built around surviving the
price war, not outgrowing it.

## Scenario analysis

- **Optimistic:** export demand absorbs excess capacity; consolidation is
  orderly; top-10 brands hold ~85% share by the window's end.
- **Base:** two more years of price compression; weaker brands exit or are
  absorbed; component suppliers keep pricing power.
- **Pessimistic:** demand plateaus while capacity stays; margin destruction
  spreads upstream; survivors are balance-sheet-selected, not product-selected.

## Dissenting view (red team)

1. The "consolidation war" frame may understate how long local-government
   support can keep marginal producers alive — exits could take far longer
   than a market-logic view predicts. `[INFERENCE] [0.6]`
2. NEV penetration ~50% is a sales-mix figure; the installed fleet is still
   majority ICE, so aftermarket and energy-infrastructure plays may matter
   more than the new-car fight this scan emphasizes. `[INFERENCE] [0.65]`
3. Export-led relief is exposed to tariff action in key destination markets;
   treating exports as the base-case absorber is optimistic. `[INFERENCE] [0.6]`
